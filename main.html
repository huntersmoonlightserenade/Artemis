Origins

Who is she? Where did her myths start?

Ἄρτεμις, goddess of hunting, the wild, children and childbirth, has a dominion over the moon. Twin sister of the god Apollon (Ἀπόλλων) and daughter of Zeus (Ζεύς) and the titaness Leto (Λητώ). She was born in Delos or Ortygia (western Turkey, near Ephesus).

It is said that when she was three years old, her father Zeus asked her what gifts she would want, so she answered:

“Give me to keep my maidenhood, Father, forever: and give me to be of many names, that Phoebus may not vie with me. And give me arrows and a bow; stay, Father, I ask thee not for quiver or for mighty bow: for me the Cyclopes will straightway fashion arrows and fashion for me a well-bent bow. But give me to be Bringer of Light and give me to gird me in a tunic with embroidered border reaching to the knee, that I may slay wild beasts. And give me sixty daughters of Oceanus for my choir – all nine years old, all maidens yet ungirdled; and give me for handmaidens twenty nymphs of Amnisus who shall tend well my buskins, and, when I shoot no more at lynx or stag, shall tend my swift hounds. And give to me all mountains; and for city, assign me any, even whatsoever thou wilt: for seldom is it that Artemis goes down to the town. On the mountains will I dwell and the cities of men I will visit only when women vexed by the sharp pang of childbirth call me to their aid even in the hour when I was born the Fates ordained that I should be their helper, forasmuch as my mother suffered no pain either when she gave me birth or when she carried me win her womb, but without travail put me from her body.” Callimachus hymn III To Artemis

She was given her silver bow by the cyclops and a pack of dogs as companions when hunting by the god Pan, this last one associated her with the wild nature and as a mistress of beasts. As it was her wish, she is one of the virgin goddesses, along with Hestia and Athena.

Myths

A list of all her available myths

Artemis & Actaeon

Actaeon was a skilled hunter, he once found Artemis showering naked in the forest and stood to watch, when the goddess realized this, she cursed him, turning him into a stag and turning his own hunting dogs against him, who tore him apart.

The Trojan war in Homer´s Iliad

The goddess's biggest role during this story happens when the king Agamemnon kills one of her sacred deer and she punishes him and his fleet by taking away the wind, leaving them unable to sail to Troy. To calm the goddess, Agamemnon sacrifices his oldest daughter Iphigenia. It is said that in the last moment, the goddess saved the young girl by substituting her with a deer during the sacrifice, then she took Iphigenia to one of her sanctuaries at Tauris.

Ancient cult

She had a small sanctuary in the Acropolis of Athens.

Artemis Ephesia was a deity of fertility with a temple in Ephesus. This version of the goddess included elements borrowed from other deities such as Isis and Cybeles.
